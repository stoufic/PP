Bears

  • Ollie
  • Mona

Bears are powerful large mammals with a distinctive shaggy coat and a short tail. Found in a variety of environments from Arctic ice fields to tropical forests, they are known for their impressive strength, keen sense of smell, and the ability to stand on their hind legs. Bears have a diverse diet that ranges from berries and plants to fish and small mammals.

Giraffes

  • Frankie
  • Coconut

Giraffes are the world's tallest mammals, known for their long necks and legs, and distinctive spotted patterns. These gentle giants roam the open grasslands in small groups and are native to Africa. Their height allows them to eat leaves from tall trees, typically acacia species, that other herbivores can't reach.

Lions

  • Mella
  • Karl

Lions, known as the 'kings of the jungle', are majestic big cats with a powerful roar and a regal mane on the males. They are the most social of the big cats, living in groups called prides. Found in grasslands, savannas, and woodlands, lions are apex predators with a diet that primarily consists of large ungulates like zebras and wildebeests.

Monkeys

  • Cookie
  • Earl
  • Bannana Pudding

Monkeys are highly social and intelligent primates with a penchant for playful behavior and vocal communication. They typically have strong limbs for climbing and grasping, and many species have prehensile tails. Monkeys are found in a variety of habitats, from jungle canopies to savannahs, and they have a varied diet including fruit, insects, and leaves.

Alligators

  • Wren
  • Aspen
  • Mika

Alligators are large, stealthy reptiles with armored bodies and powerful jaws. They are freshwater creatures that are often found in slow-moving rivers, swamps, and marshes. Alligators are opportunistic feeders whose diet includes fish, birds, and mammals, and they play a crucial role in their ecosystem as apex predators.
